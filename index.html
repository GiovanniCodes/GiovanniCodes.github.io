Feel Good Storys

  • Through Rise Against Hunger Philippines’ Farm to Fork initiative, we are implementing contract farming, which involves the hiring of small-scale indigenous farmers to provide vegetables for use in Rise Against Hunger meals. With this strategy in place, farmers grow food to support their families, and excess harvest is consolidated and sold at fair market prices to the Negrese Volunteers for Change Foundation, which then dehydrates the vegetables for Rise Against Hunger’s meals.
  • In a small town Bas Andre outside of Gonaives,Haiti, Rise Againist Hunger hired a community faming group led by Telson to teach traditional farming techniques once a week to adults in Bas Andre. These class not only empower farmers but women in the community who are inspired to do more. The mothers alo bring there children to the meetings.
