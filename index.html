Virtual Reality For Social Good

Type 2 Diabetes Education targeting Prediabetic Young adults

  • More than 30 millionAmericans have diabetes (about 1 in 10)
  • 90% to 95% of them have type 2 diabetes.
  • Type 2 diabetes most often develops in people over age 45, but more and more children, teens, and young adults are also developing it.

Plot

You start in your house after waking up to start the day. You are hungry but then realize there is nothing much to eat after checking the fridge. You then hear your phone vibrating and answer it. Your friend reminds you that you have to meet up with him to eat, his treat. Your friend set up the food without giving you the option and this is where your decisions are tested. As you get up and ready to go, your friend is reminded that he has to go do groceries and you realize that you also so need to fill up your fridge. You then check out the store and shop around until your cart is full. When you get home you realize you are hungry and check out the fridge which contain the items you bought. You eat and you head back to sleep. This experience forces the user to take account what they eat and to maintian the proper blood sugar. If the user doesn't maintain the correct blood sugar throug the experience then symptoms induced by being diabetic takes place such as blurred vision for high blood sugar and lost of energy for low blood sugar.
